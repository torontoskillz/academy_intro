Vision Statement

We aim to be seen as the leader in Ontario's soccer landscape in producing players of both excellent skill and character on and off the field.

Mission Statement

We aim to provide relentless leadership and mentorship to our players in their efforts to carry excellence in their abilities in soccer and life.

The Toronto Skillz Philosophy

Built on a continued education, we develop our players to understand roles and responsibilities within our system, creating a holistic atmosphere in our developmental success.

In possession, we encourage play out from the back, progressing to the attacking third to create chances with creativity, variety, and fearlessness. We teach our players to have fast and intelligent intent in our movement, passing, and dribbling.
When not in possession we are teaching the zonal-man and 11 defenders philosophy. Additionally, we teach—as a team, unit, and individual—when to and when not to be aggressive to win possession.
Our year-round program consists of team and individual practice, playful activities to hone developed skills, and match play to use developed skills in competitive environments. Our training during the off-season is crucial to maximize development as players enter their primes.

Player Performance Path

At Toronto Skillz, we provide a clear path for our players to achieve their goals. From Bundesliga/German academy opportunities to the big Canadian clubs, the possibilties are endless.

Today, we execute in the present, improve in the moment to be great.

Tomorrow, we learn from yesterday. We do not repeat our failures and aim to be better in the future.

Beyond, we educate for the future and challenge the status quo with the intent to be more.

Quality & Quantity Combined

Toronto Skillz develops players with a long-term perspective. Constant practice and play is important which is why our season is a full year. However, we also value the importance of rest and recovery between and during matches and practices.

Coach

Our coaches come from all walks of life, and most of them have played at the professional level. They are accredited by the Union of European Football Association (UEFA), the United States Soccer Federation (USSF), United States Youth Soccer (USYS), United Soccer Coaches (USC) Canada Soccer, and Ontario Soccer.

Train

Through our year-round program, we are constantly training to ingrain the fundamentals into muscle memory. With our partners at Schaechter Sports, we also bring top German youth coaches to teach our players during our German ID camps.

Evaluate

To ensure our lessons have gotten through to each player, we evaluate them in training and during games. With our partners at MSA Football, video analysis and match statistics are provided to coaches starting from the U15 level. From there, we can look and the positives of each individual or team along with highlight things that need to be improved.

Feedback

With all the information we have gathered, we give constructive feedback to our players with the trust that they will focus on making the needed improvements. We check in with players on their physical and mental health.

Plan

We revise or repeat personalized training regimens for the players based on evaluations and feedback. We adjust accordingly and get ready to come full circle as we continue coaching the players to the best of our abilities.
